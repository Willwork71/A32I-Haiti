A32i Country Report: Haiti

Government Readiness

Haiti is beginning to explore AI as a tool for long-term national resilience and international development partnerships. While infrastructure remains a key challenge, Haiti’s leadership has expressed openness to innovation. As former Prime Minister Ariel Henry stated, “Digital transformation is a necessary step toward our national recovery.” The government has taken steps to modernize governance systems through partnerships with the UN and regional tech hubs, signaling potential for AI-based progress.

Projected Financial Impact

With 30% integration, A32i systems could deliver $240–$320 million in annual value for Haiti—representing up to 18.8% of total government spending. Primary benefits include $120M in smart public budgeting, $80M in sustainable food systems, and $60M in targeted social programs. With modest setup costs and foundational tech already underway, ROI could be achieved in under 30 months.
